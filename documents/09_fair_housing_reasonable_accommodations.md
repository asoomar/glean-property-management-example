# Fair Housing and Reasonable Accommodation Policy

Northstar Residential Management is committed to equal housing opportunity and compliance with federal, state, and local fair housing laws. This document provides operational guidance for leasing, resident service, maintenance, and management teams.

## Equal Housing Opportunity

Northstar does not discriminate based on protected characteristics, including:

- Race
- Color
- Religion
- Sex
- National origin
- Disability
- Familial status
- Source of income where protected by local law
- Sexual orientation where protected by local law
- Gender identity where protected by local law
- Age where protected by local law
- Marital status where protected by local law
- Any other locally protected class

Staff must apply policies consistently and avoid different treatment based on protected characteristics.

## Consistent Leasing Practices

Leasing staff must offer available apartments based on the applicant’s stated preferences, budget, timing, and availability.

Staff may not steer applicants to or away from certain:

- Buildings
- Floors
- Unit types
- Communities
- Neighborhoods
- Amenity areas
- School zones

Staff should avoid statements that imply a community is better or worse for a protected group.

## Occupancy and Familial Status

Northstar uses neutral occupancy standards and applies them consistently.

Staff may not discourage families with children from applying for upper floors, pool-facing units, or certain buildings.

Rules involving children must be based on safety or property operations and cannot unreasonably restrict families.

## Reasonable Accommodation Requests

A reasonable accommodation is a change to a rule, policy, practice, or service that may be necessary for a person with a disability to use and enjoy housing.

Examples:

- Assistance animal
- Reserved accessible parking space
- Rent reminder accommodation
- Communication by email instead of phone
- Live-in aide request
- Transfer to accessible unit
- Extra time to provide documents due to disability-related need
- Permission to install accessibility equipment

Residents and applicants do not need to use specific words like “reasonable accommodation” to make a request.

## Reasonable Modification Requests

A reasonable modification is a physical change to the apartment or common area that may be necessary because of a disability.

Examples:

- Grab bars
- Lever door handles
- Ramp
- Lowered peephole
- Visual doorbell
- Roll-in shower modification
- Widened doorway
- Cabinet modification

Approval, cost responsibility, permits, licensed contractor requirements, and restoration obligations depend on applicable law and property requirements.

## Accommodation Request Process

Standard process:

1. Staff receives request.
2. Staff documents date, request, resident/applicant name, and property.
3. Staff sends acknowledgment within 3 business days.
4. Compliance team reviews request.
5. If needed and legally allowed, reliable documentation is requested.
6. Decision is issued in writing.
7. Approved accommodation is documented in the resident file.
8. Relevant staff are notified only on a need-to-know basis.

Staff should not ignore verbal requests. If a request is made verbally, staff should document it and offer the written request form, but the request does not need to be on a specific form to be considered.

## Documentation Standards

When disability and disability-related need are obvious, staff should not request additional documentation.

When not obvious, Northstar may request reliable documentation from a qualified third party or other legally acceptable source.

Staff may not ask for:

- Medical diagnosis
- Detailed medical records
- Medication list
- Invasive medical history
- Proof beyond what is legally allowed

## Assistance Animals

Assistance animals are not pets. Approved assistance animals are exempt from pet fees, pet deposits, pet rent, breed restrictions, and weight restrictions.

Residents with assistance animals are responsible for:

- Animal control
- Waste cleanup
- Damage caused by the animal
- Excessive noise
- Compliance with vaccination and licensing laws
- Preventing direct threats to others

A request may be denied or limited if the animal poses a direct threat, would cause substantial property damage, or creates an undue burden, subject to legal review.

## Parking Accommodation

A resident may request a reserved accessible parking space as a reasonable accommodation.

The request should be routed to compliance and property management. If approved, staff should document:

- Location of assigned space
- Signage or marking needed
- Effective date
- Whether parking fee changes are required
- Communication sent to resident

Staff should not promise immediate approval before review.

## Service Standards for Staff

Staff should use neutral, respectful language.

Staff should avoid:

- Asking whether someone is disabled
- Commenting on visible or invisible disabilities
- Discussing a resident’s accommodation with neighbors
- Retaliating against a resident for requesting accommodation
- Making jokes or assumptions about protected characteristics
- Giving legal opinions

## Complaint Escalation

Fair housing complaints must be escalated to the regional manager and compliance team the same business day.

Examples requiring escalation:

- Allegation of discrimination
- Complaint involving disability accommodation
- Assistance animal dispute
- Source of income complaint
- Familial status complaint
- Staff misconduct allegation
- Threatened fair housing complaint
- Agency or attorney letter

Staff should preserve documents, emails, notes, call logs, photos, and notices related to the complaint.

## Record Retention

Accommodation requests, decisions, supporting documentation, and related communications should be stored in the resident file or compliance system according to Northstar’s record retention policy.

Sensitive documentation should be limited to staff with a business need to know.
