# Community Rules and Lease Violations

Northstar Residential Management maintains community rules to support safety, cleanliness, resident comfort, and property operations. Residents are responsible for their own conduct and for the conduct of occupants, guests, vendors, and invitees.

## Quiet Hours

Standard quiet hours are 10:00 PM to 8:00 AM Sunday through Thursday and 11:00 PM to 9:00 AM Friday and Saturday.

During quiet hours, residents should avoid:

- Loud music
- Parties
- Yelling
- Loud television or gaming
- Slamming doors
- Excessive pet noise
- Loud vehicle noise
- Balcony or patio gatherings that disturb others

Noise complaints should include date, time, location, description, and any available recording or witness information.

## Guest Policy

Guests are allowed, but residents are responsible for them.

A guest may not stay more than 7 consecutive days or more than 14 total days in a 60-day period without written approval.

A guest who exceeds the limit may be considered an unauthorized occupant. Unauthorized occupants may result in a lease violation and a $200 unauthorized occupant fee.

## Parking Rules

Parking rules vary by community, but standard rules include:

- Residents must display valid parking permit if required.
- Vehicles must be operable and legally registered.
- Vehicles may not block garages, fire lanes, dumpsters, sidewalks, access gates, or reserved spaces.
- Visitor parking is for guests only.
- Commercial vehicles require management approval.
- Vehicle repairs are not allowed in parking areas, except emergency tire changes.
- Vehicles leaking fluids may be required to move or be removed.

Towing may occur for fire lane violations, blocked access, expired tags, unauthorized reserved parking, inoperable vehicles, or repeat parking violations.

## Trash and Bulk Items

Residents must place trash in designated containers, compactors, chutes, or valet trash containers according to property rules.

Prohibited trash behavior:

- Leaving trash in hallways
- Leaving bags outside doors outside approved valet hours
- Dumping furniture without approval
- Placing loose trash beside compactor
- Disposing of hazardous materials
- Leaving boxes unbroken in trash rooms
- Using community dumpsters for business waste

Fees:

- First trash violation: $50
- Repeat trash violation: $100
- Bulk item removal: actual cost plus $50 administrative fee

## Balconies and Patios

Balconies and patios must be kept clean and may not be used for storage.

Prohibited items may include:

- Indoor furniture
- Trash
- Clothing or towels over railings
- Appliances
- Bicycles where prohibited
- Charcoal grills
- Propane grills where prohibited
- Litter boxes
- Unsightly storage
- Holiday decorations left up more than 14 days after holiday

Plants must have water trays and may not drain onto lower apartments.

## Smoking Policy

Smoking rules depend on the community. At smoke-free communities, smoking is prohibited inside apartments, balconies, patios, amenity areas, breezeways, and common areas.

Smoking includes cigarettes, cigars, vaping, marijuana, and any similar substance unless local law requires a different treatment.

Smoking violations may result in cleaning charges, lease violations, odor remediation charges, and non-renewal.

Typical smoke remediation charges range from $350 to $1,500 depending on severity.

## Amenities

Residents may use amenities during posted hours. Standard amenity rules:

- No glass in pool areas
- No unsupervised minors where rules require adult supervision
- No parties without reservation
- No alcohol where prohibited
- No pets in pool or fitness areas except service animals
- Clean equipment after use
- Follow occupancy limits
- Do not prop open access doors

Amenity privileges may be suspended for misuse, damage, or rule violations.

## Package Lockers and Deliveries

Residents are responsible for collecting packages promptly. Northstar is not responsible for lost, stolen, damaged, or misdelivered packages unless required by law.

Package locker violations include:

- Sharing access codes
- Leaving oversized packages in office beyond deadline
- Using package rooms for storage
- Damaging lockers
- Picking up packages addressed to others

## Lease Violation Process

Standard lease enforcement process:

1. Incident reported or observed.
2. Staff documents issue with photos, witness statements, system logs, or written notes.
3. Manager reviews documentation.
4. Warning or lease violation notice is issued.
5. Resident is given required cure period if applicable.
6. Repeat or severe violations are escalated.
7. Legal action, non-renewal, or termination may be pursued if allowed.

Severe issues may skip warning and go directly to formal notice or legal review.

## Standard Violation Fees

Fees vary by lease and law, but standard administrative fines are:

| Violation | First Fee | Repeat Fee |
|---|---:|---:|
| Trash violation | $50 | $100 |
| Unauthorized pet | $250 | $250 plus lease action |
| Unauthorized occupant | $200 | $200 plus lease action |
| Noise violation | Warning | $75 |
| Parking violation | Warning or tow | Tow plus admin fee |
| Amenity misuse | $75 | $150 |
| Smoking violation | $250 | Actual remediation plus lease action |
| Failure to clean pet waste | $75 | $150 |

## Prohibited Conduct

Residents, occupants, and guests may not engage in:

- Threats or harassment
- Violence
- Illegal activity
- Drug-related criminal activity
- Property damage
- Tampering with cameras, gates, locks, or safety devices
- Interfering with staff or vendors
- Misuse of emergency systems
- Firearms misuse or threats
- Disturbing other residents
- Retaliation against complainants

## Documentation Standards for Staff

When staff documents a violation, the record should include:

- Date and time
- Apartment number
- People involved if known
- Description of issue
- Photos or video if available
- Witness names if available
- Prior related violations
- Action taken
- Staff initials

Accurate documentation is required before charging fees or escalating enforcement.
