# Property Management Operations SOP

This document provides standard operating procedures for Northstar Residential Management’s apartment community operations. It is intended for property managers, assistant managers, leasing staff, maintenance supervisors, and regional managers.

## Daily Office Opening Checklist

Before opening the leasing office, staff should complete:

1. Unlock office and amenity areas scheduled to open.
2. Check voicemail and emergency maintenance messages.
3. Review new online applications.
4. Review move-ins scheduled for the next 7 days.
5. Review delinquency dashboard.
6. Check package room or locker exceptions.
7. Review tour appointments.
8. Confirm model and tour path are clean.
9. Check key inventory.
10. Review unresolved resident escalations.

Any urgent issue should be escalated to the property manager by 10:00 AM.

## Leasing Follow-Up Standards

New leads should receive follow-up within the following timelines:

| Lead Source | First Response Target |
|---|---|
| Website inquiry | 15 minutes during business hours |
| Phone missed call | 30 minutes during business hours |
| ILS inquiry | 2 business hours |
| Walk-in guest | Immediate |
| After-hours lead | By 10:00 AM next business day |

Minimum follow-up cadence for active prospects:

- Day 0: call, email, and text
- Day 1: call and email
- Day 3: text and email
- Day 5: call
- Day 7: final follow-up unless prospect remains active

Staff should document all follow-up attempts in the CRM.

## Tour Process

For each tour, leasing staff should:

1. Confirm appointment time.
2. Ask about move-in date, budget, unit size, pets, and must-haves.
3. Verify government ID before touring.
4. Show available unit or model.
5. Explain application process and fees.
6. Send quote before end of day.
7. Follow up within 2 hours after tour.

Tour notes should include preferences, objections, desired unit, timeline, and next step.

## Application Review SOP

Applications should be reviewed at least twice daily: once before noon and once before 4:00 PM.

Staff should verify:

- All adults applied
- Application fees paid
- ID uploaded
- Income documents uploaded
- Unit is still available
- Holding fee paid
- Screening result received
- Conditional items requested
- Approval or denial communication sent

Applications with missing information should receive a written request listing exactly what is needed and the deadline.

## Move-In Audit

The move-in file should be audited 2 business days before move-in.

Audit checklist:

- Lease signed by all residents
- Addenda signed
- Move-in funds paid
- Renters insurance verified
- Utilities confirmed
- Pet documents complete
- Parking registration complete
- Key packet prepared
- Unit made ready
- Maintenance make-ready complete
- Final unit walk completed
- Welcome email sent

The property manager or assistant manager must approve the move-in file before key release.

## Make-Ready Standards

Maintenance should complete make-ready work before the scheduled move-in date.

Make-ready checklist:

- Paint completed
- Flooring cleaned or replaced
- Appliances tested
- HVAC tested
- Plumbing checked
- Lights and outlets checked
- Smoke detectors tested
- Locks changed or rekeyed
- Blinds installed
- Cabinets and doors adjusted
- Pest treatment completed if needed
- Final clean completed
- Photos uploaded

The leasing team should not promise a unit is ready until maintenance confirms completion.

## Resident Escalation Process

Resident complaints should be logged and categorized.

Escalation levels:

| Level | Example | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Level 1 | Basic question, routine request | Leasing or maintenance staff |
| Level 2 | Repeated issue, unresolved work order, billing dispute | Assistant manager |
| Level 3 | Legal threat, fair housing issue, severe complaint | Property manager |
| Level 4 | Media threat, attorney letter, agency complaint, safety incident | Regional manager and compliance |

Level 3 and Level 4 issues should be documented in writing the same business day.

## Delinquency Review

The property manager should review delinquency every business day from the 4th through the 15th of the month.

Daily review includes:

- Total delinquent balance
- Residents owing over $500
- Residents with failed payments
- Residents on payment plans
- Notices issued
- Legal referrals pending
- Move-outs with balances
- Subsidy or voucher payment delays

Staff should avoid informal promises that change collection status unless documented.

## Maintenance Operations

Maintenance supervisors should review open work orders daily.

Daily review includes:

- Emergency work orders
- Open urgent requests
- Work orders older than 5 business days
- Parts on order
- Vendor scheduling
- Resident access issues
- Repeat work orders
- Make-ready status
- Preventive maintenance calendar

Maintenance notes should be clear enough for office staff to answer resident questions.

## Vendor Management

Vendors must provide:

- W-9
- Certificate of insurance
- Signed vendor agreement if required
- Scope of work
- Pricing or approved estimate
- Completion photos when requested

Work over $1,000 requires property manager approval. Work over $5,000 requires regional manager approval unless emergency approval is documented.

## Incident Reporting

Incident reports are required for:

- Fire
- Flood
- Injury
- Crime
- Police activity
- Aggressive animal incident
- Major property damage
- Employee injury
- Resident threat
- Fair housing complaint
- Media or attorney contact

Incident reports should be submitted the same business day.

## Weekly Property Manager Review

Each week, the property manager should review:

- Occupancy
- Exposure units
- Upcoming renewals
- Notices to vacate
- Delinquency
- Work order aging
- Make-ready aging
- Leasing traffic
- Application pipeline
- Online reviews
- Resident escalations
- Budget variances

The regional manager may request a weekly summary every Monday by 12:00 PM.

## Monthly Close Process

At month-end, the property manager should review:

- Rent collections
- Bad debt
- Concessions
- Move-ins
- Move-outs
- Deposit accounting
- Vendor invoices
- Utility bills
- Renewal performance
- Occupancy
- Delinquency write-offs
- Resident credits
- Open accounting issues

Accounting corrections should be submitted before the monthly close deadline.
