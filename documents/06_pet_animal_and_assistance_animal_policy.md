# Pet, Animal, and Assistance Animal Policy

Northstar Residential Management allows approved pets at many apartment communities. All animals must be disclosed, screened, and approved before they are brought onto the property, except as otherwise required by law for assistance animals.

## Pet Screening Requirement

All residents must complete an animal profile during the application process, even if they do not own a pet. Residents without pets complete a no-pet profile.

Pet owners must provide:

- Animal name
- Species
- Breed
- Age
- Weight
- Color
- Photo
- Vaccination records
- City or county license if required
- Emergency contact for the animal

Pet profiles must be updated annually.

## Standard Pet Fees

Unless property-specific rules state otherwise, standard pet charges are:

| Charge | Amount |
|---|---:|
| Pet screening fee | $25 per pet profile |
| One-time pet fee | $300 per pet |
| Refundable pet deposit | $250 per pet |
| Monthly pet rent | $35 per pet |
| Unauthorized pet fee | $250 per occurrence |
| Pet waste violation | $75 first violation, $150 repeat |
| Pet damage | Actual cost of repair |

Pet fees and pet rent do not apply to approved assistance animals.

## Pet Limits

Most communities allow up to 2 pets per apartment.

Aquariums are limited to 20 gallons unless management approves a larger tank in writing. Aquarium insurance or additional deposit may be required.

Exotic animals, livestock, venomous animals, and illegal animals are not permitted.

## Weight and Breed Restrictions

Pet restrictions vary by property and local law.

Where permitted, restricted dog breeds may include:

- Pit Bull Terrier
- Staffordshire Terrier
- Rottweiler
- Doberman Pinscher
- Chow Chow
- Akita
- Presa Canario
- Wolf hybrid
- Any mix including a restricted breed

Breed restrictions do not apply to approved assistance animals, but assistance animals must not pose a direct threat or cause substantial property damage.

## Visiting Pets and Pet Sitting

Visiting pets are not allowed without written approval. Pet sitting is treated the same as having a pet in the apartment and requires prior approval.

Unauthorized visiting pets may result in fees and lease violation notices.

## Pet Rules

Pet owners must:

- Keep pets on a leash in common areas
- Clean up pet waste immediately
- Use designated pet areas when provided
- Prevent excessive barking or noise
- Prevent aggressive behavior
- Prevent damage to landscaping, doors, flooring, and common areas
- Keep pets out of pool areas, fitness centers, offices, and clubrooms unless allowed
- Follow vaccination and licensing requirements

## Pet Waste Enforcement

Properties may use pet DNA registration or waste monitoring.

If pet waste is traced to a resident’s animal, charges may include:

- Waste removal fee
- DNA testing fee
- Lease violation fine
- Repeat violation fee

Three pet waste violations within a 12-month period may result in pet removal or non-renewal.

## Pet Noise Complaints

Pet noise complaints are reviewed by the leasing office. Residents may be asked to provide corrective action, such as training, schedule changes, pet daycare, or noise mitigation.

Repeated verified complaints may result in lease violation notices.

## Assistance Animal Requests

Service animals and assistance animals are not pets. Residents may request a reasonable accommodation for an assistance animal at any time.

The request process may include:

1. Resident submits accommodation request.
2. Leasing office acknowledges within 3 business days.
3. Compliance team reviews request.
4. Reliable documentation may be requested if disability-related need is not obvious.
5. Decision is provided in writing.
6. Approved animal is added to the resident file.

Northstar does not charge pet rent, pet fees, or pet deposits for approved assistance animals.

## Assistance Animal Conduct

Residents with assistance animals are still responsible for animal behavior, waste cleanup, damage, noise, and control of the animal.

An assistance animal may be denied or removed if it poses a direct threat, causes substantial property damage, or creates an undue burden, subject to applicable law.

## Animal Bites or Aggressive Incidents

Any bite, attempted bite, attack, or aggressive incident must be reported to the leasing office immediately.

Management may require:

- Incident report
- Animal control report
- Removal of animal
- Additional documentation
- Legal or compliance review

Failure to report an aggressive animal incident may result in lease enforcement.
