# Move-Out, Inspections, and Deposit Accounting

This document describes Northstar Residential Management’s move-out process, final inspection standards, security deposit accounting, and common move-out charges.

## Notice to Vacate

Residents must provide written notice to vacate according to the lease. The standard requirement is 60 days’ written notice.

The notice must include:

- Resident name
- Apartment number
- Intended move-out date
- Forwarding address if available
- Contact information
- Resident signature or portal submission

A resident who gives less than the required notice may be charged insufficient notice rent through the required notice period.

## Move-Out Confirmation

After notice is received, the leasing office sends move-out instructions within 2 business days.

The instructions include:

- Move-out date
- Key return deadline
- Cleaning expectations
- Utility transfer instructions
- Final inspection process
- Deposit accounting timeline
- Forwarding address request
- Pre-move-out inspection availability

## Pre-Move-Out Inspection

Residents may request a pre-move-out inspection between 10 and 30 days before the move-out date.

The inspection helps identify obvious cleaning or repair items. It is not a final inspection and does not guarantee final charges.

Staff should note visible issues such as:

- Carpet stains
- Wall damage
- Missing blinds
- Pet damage
- Appliance damage
- Trash or furniture removal
- Heavy cleaning needs

## Key Return

Residents must return all keys, fobs, remotes, mailbox keys, and parking permits by 12:00 PM on the move-out date unless the lease states another time.

Possession is not considered returned until all keys and access devices are returned.

Failure to return keys may result in additional rent, lock change charges, and replacement charges.

## Cleaning Standards

Residents should leave the apartment clean and free of trash, furniture, and personal belongings.

Expected cleaning includes:

- Remove all belongings
- Clean kitchen counters
- Clean cabinets and drawers
- Clean refrigerator and freezer
- Clean oven and stovetop
- Clean microwave
- Clean bathrooms
- Sweep and mop hard floors
- Vacuum carpet
- Remove trash
- Remove balcony or patio items
- Replace burned-out bulbs if required by lease

## Damage vs Normal Wear and Tear

Normal wear and tear is ordinary deterioration from normal use. Damage is harm beyond ordinary use.

Examples of normal wear and tear:

- Minor carpet traffic wear
- Small nail holes
- Faded paint
- Minor scuffs
- Loose door handle from normal use

Examples of damage:

- Large wall holes
- Broken doors
- Pet urine damage
- Burn marks
- Missing fixtures
- Broken blinds
- Stained carpet
- Cracked countertops
- Unauthorized paint
- Water damage from failure to report leak

## Carpet and Flooring Proration

When carpet replacement is required due to resident damage, Northstar may prorate the charge based on carpet age and expected useful life unless local law or property policy requires a different method.

Standard carpet useful life is 5 years.

Example:

- Carpet replacement cost: $1,200
- Carpet age: 2 years
- Remaining useful life: 3 years
- Resident damage charge: 60% of cost = $720

Pet urine, burns, dye stains, and large permanent stains may require replacement.

## Final Utility Charges

Final utility charges may not be available at the time of move-out inspection. Utility charges may be added to the final account statement after bills are received.

Residents remain responsible for utilities through the possession return date or lease responsibility date.

## Deposit Accounting Timeline

Northstar prepares the final account statement within the timeframe required by state or local law. The standard internal target is 21 days after move-out, but legal deadlines control.

The final statement includes:

- Security deposit
- Unpaid rent
- Fees
- Utilities
- Cleaning charges
- Damage charges
- Key or access device charges
- Deposit refund or balance due

Refunds are mailed or electronically sent to the forwarding address or payment method on file.

## Disputing Move-Out Charges

Residents may dispute final charges in writing within 30 days of receiving the final statement.

A dispute should include:

- Resident name
- Former apartment number
- Specific charge disputed
- Reason for dispute
- Photos or documents
- Desired correction

Northstar reviews disputes within 10 business days when possible.

## Abandoned Property

Items left after possession is returned may be considered abandoned property and handled according to the lease and local law.

Storage, removal, and disposal costs may be charged to the resident.
