# Standard Lease Terms

Northstar Residential Management uses a standard lease package for apartment homes across its managed communities. This document describes the default lease terms used at most Northstar-managed properties. Property-specific lease terms, local regulations, and signed lease documents always control if there is a conflict.

## Standard Lease Lengths

Most apartment homes are offered on 12-month lease terms. Depending on availability, seasonality, and owner approval, the leasing office may offer the following terms:

| Lease Term | Typical Pricing Treatment |
|---|---|
| 6 months | Market rent plus $150/month short-term premium |
| 9 months | Market rent plus $75/month short-term premium |
| 12 months | Standard advertised market rent |
| 13 to 15 months | May qualify for preferred pricing if available |
| Month-to-month | Market rent plus $300/month premium after lease expiration |

Month-to-month tenancy is not guaranteed. It requires written approval from the property manager and must be confirmed before the lease expiration date.

## Rent Due Date

Rent is due on the 1st day of each month. Rent is considered late if it is not received by 11:59 PM local property time on the 3rd day of the month.

Rent is not considered paid until funds are successfully processed. A pending ACH or card transaction that later fails is treated as unpaid.

## Rent Payment Methods

Residents may pay rent through the resident portal using ACH, debit card, or credit card. ACH is the preferred method.

Accepted payment methods:

- ACH through resident portal
- Debit card through resident portal
- Credit card through resident portal
- Cashier’s check, only when approved by management
- Money order, only when approved by management

Cash is not accepted at the leasing office.

Credit card and debit card convenience fees are charged by the payment processor and are not controlled by Northstar.

## Late Fees

Unless local law requires otherwise, the standard late fee structure is:

- Initial late fee: $95 if rent is unpaid after the 3rd day of the month
- Daily late fee: $15 per day beginning on the 4th day of the month
- Maximum monthly late fee: 10% of monthly base rent

Late fees are not waived because of travel, email issues, bank delays, portal password issues, or failure to receive a reminder.

The property manager may waive a late fee only when there is a verified property-side error or documented payment processor issue.

## Returned Payments

Returned payments include failed ACH transfers, chargebacks, insufficient funds, closed accounts, or disputed transactions.

Standard returned payment fees:

- First returned payment in a 12-month period: $50
- Second returned payment in a 12-month period: $75
- Third returned payment in a 12-month period: resident must pay by certified funds for the next 6 months

If a returned payment causes rent to become late, late fees may also apply.

## Security Deposit

The standard security deposit is based on the screening result:

| Screening Result | Deposit Requirement |
|---|---|
| Approved | $500 deposit |
| Conditional approval - moderate risk | One month’s rent deposit |
| Conditional approval - elevated risk | 1.5 months’ rent deposit or approved guarantor |
| Deposit alternative approved | Monthly deposit waiver fee may apply |

The security deposit is not the last month’s rent and cannot be applied to rent before move-out without written approval.

## Administrative and Move-In Fees

The standard administrative fee is $175 per apartment. This fee covers application processing administration, lease preparation, resident account setup, and move-in coordination.

The standard application fee is $65 per adult applicant. Each adult age 18 or older must apply separately.

A unit holding fee of $250 may be required to reserve an apartment home. If the applicant is approved and moves in, the holding fee is credited toward move-in costs. If the applicant cancels after approval, the holding fee is forfeited unless local law requires a different result.

## Concessions and Specials

Move-in specials may include reduced rent, waived fees, or one-time concessions. Any concession must be written in the lease or concession addendum.

If a resident breaks the lease early, is evicted, skips, or fails to complete the agreed lease term, Northstar may require repayment of concessions according to the concession addendum.

Example: If a resident receives one month free on a 12-month lease and moves out after 5 months without an approved early termination, the free month concession may become immediately due.

## Early Termination

Residents who request early lease termination must give written notice and receive written instructions from the leasing office.

The standard early termination option requires:

- 60 days’ written notice
- Payment of an early termination fee equal to 2 months’ base rent
- Repayment of concessions, if applicable
- Payment of all unpaid rent, utilities, damages, and other charges
- Return of keys and possession by the approved date

Early termination is not complete until all required payments are made and possession is returned.

Residents who move out without following the early termination process remain responsible for lease obligations to the extent allowed by law.

## Lease Break Without Approved Early Termination

If a resident vacates before the lease end date without an approved early termination agreement, charges may include:

- Rent through the lease end date or until the unit is re-rented, depending on applicable law
- Advertising and re-leasing costs where permitted
- Concession chargebacks
- Utilities
- Damages
- Cleaning
- Legal fees and collection costs where permitted

## Notice to Vacate

The standard notice requirement is 60 days’ written notice. Notice must be submitted through the resident portal or by using the official Notice to Vacate form.

Verbal notice, text messages to staff, phone calls, or informal emails do not count unless the property manager confirms acceptance in writing.

Notice must include:

- Resident name
- Apartment number
- Intended move-out date
- Forwarding address, if known
- Reason for move-out, optional
- Resident signature or portal confirmation

## Renewal Process

Renewal offers are typically sent 75 days before lease expiration. Residents should respond at least 60 days before lease expiration.

Renewal pricing is based on current market rent, lease term selected, account standing, and owner direction.

A resident may be ineligible for renewal if there are repeated late payments, unresolved lease violations, unauthorized occupants, unauthorized animals, significant disturbances, property damage, or unpaid balances.

## Occupancy Standards

Unless local law requires a different standard, Northstar uses a two-person-per-bedroom plus one additional occupant guideline.

Examples:

- Studio: up to 2 occupants
- 1 bedroom: up to 3 occupants
- 2 bedroom: up to 5 occupants
- 3 bedroom: up to 7 occupants

All occupants must be disclosed. Anyone age 18 or older must apply, be screened, and be listed on the lease.

## Transfer Policy

Residents may request a transfer to another apartment at the same community after completing at least 6 months of the lease term.

Transfer requirements:

- Account must be current
- No active lease violations
- New application screening may be required
- Transfer fee: $350
- New lease must be signed before keys are released
- Current unit must pass move-out inspection

A transfer is not approved until management provides written approval.

## Lease Addenda

Lease packages may include addenda for:

- Community rules
- Utility billing
- Pest control
- Parking
- Package lockers
- Pet policy
- Assistance animal accommodation
- Renters insurance
- Mold prevention
- Bed bug reporting
- Smoke-free community rules
- Amenity rules
- Smart lock or access device rules

Residents are responsible for complying with all signed addenda.
