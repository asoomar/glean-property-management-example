# Application and Screening Criteria

This document explains Northstar Residential Management’s application requirements, screening standards, and approval conditions for apartment applicants. The criteria are intended to be applied consistently across managed communities, subject to property-specific rules and applicable law.

## Application Requirement

Every adult age 18 or older who will live in the apartment must submit a separate application. This includes spouses, partners, roommates, adult children, adult relatives, and any other adult occupant.

Each applicant must provide:

- Legal name
- Date of birth
- Social Security number or approved alternative identity documentation
- Government-issued photo identification
- Current address
- Prior addresses for the past 3 years
- Employment or income information
- Rental history
- Authorization for credit, criminal, eviction, and rental screening where permitted

Incomplete applications are not reviewed until missing information is received.

## Fees Due at Application

The following fees are typically due when applying:

| Fee | Amount | Refundable? |
|---|---:|---|
| Application fee | $65 per adult | No |
| Administrative fee | $175 per apartment | No after approval |
| Holding fee | $250 per apartment | Credited at move-in if approved and lease is signed |
| Pet screening fee | $25 per pet profile | No |

If the applicant is denied, the holding fee is refunded within 10 business days unless local law or the application agreement requires a different timeline.

If the applicant is approved but cancels, fails to sign the lease, or fails to provide required documents by the deadline, the holding fee is forfeited.

## Application Timeline

Typical processing time is 1 to 3 business days after all required information is submitted.

Applications may take longer when:

- Income cannot be verified automatically
- Employer verification is delayed
- Landlord references do not respond
- Identity verification fails
- Additional documentation is requested
- The applicant has credit freezes or fraud alerts
- Guarantor review is required
- Compliance review is needed

## Income Requirement

Applicants must have combined gross monthly income equal to at least 3 times the monthly base rent.

Example: If rent is $1,600 per month, the household must show at least $4,800 in gross monthly income.

For guarantors, the income requirement is 5 times monthly base rent.

Example: If rent is $1,600 per month, the guarantor must show at least $8,000 in gross monthly income.

## Acceptable Income Documentation

Acceptable income documents include:

- Two most recent pay stubs
- Offer letter on company letterhead with start date and salary
- Last 3 months of bank statements for self-employed applicants
- Most recent tax return for self-employed applicants
- Social Security award letter
- Disability benefit letter
- Retirement or pension statement
- Court-ordered support documentation
- Housing voucher documentation
- Trust or investment income statement

Screenshots, handwritten notes, unverifiable cash income, or edited documents are not accepted.

## Employment Verification

For employed applicants, Northstar may verify employment through pay stubs, employer contact, payroll verification systems, or offer letters.

New employment may be accepted if the offer letter includes:

- Employer name
- Applicant name
- Position
- Start date within 45 days of application
- Compensation amount
- HR or manager contact information

If the applicant has not started the job, additional deposit or guarantor approval may be required.

## Self-Employment Review

Self-employed applicants must provide documentation showing stable income. Northstar may request:

- Most recent tax return
- Profit and loss statement
- 3 months of business bank statements
- 3 months of personal bank statements
- Active business registration
- Client contracts or recurring revenue documentation

Self-employed income may be averaged over the documentation period.

## Credit Review

Northstar reviews credit history using a third-party screening provider. The review may include:

- Credit score range
- Payment history
- Collections
- Charge-offs
- Bankruptcies
- Debt-to-income indicators
- Housing-related debt
- Fraud alerts or identity concerns

Credit score alone does not determine approval, but it influences the approval condition.

General credit result guidance:

| Screening Result | Typical Outcome |
|---|---|
| Strong credit profile, no housing debt | Approved |
| Moderate credit concerns, no major housing debt | Conditional approval |
| Weak credit profile or recent collections | Higher deposit or guarantor |
| Unpaid landlord debt or recent eviction judgment | Denial or manager review |

## Rental History

Applicants should provide at least 24 months of rental or housing history where available.

Northstar may contact prior landlords to verify:

- Payment history
- Lease violations
- Noise complaints
- Property damage
- Skips or abandonment
- Eviction filings
- Proper notice to vacate
- Balance owed

A prior landlord balance above $500 generally requires proof of payment or written payment arrangement before approval.

A prior eviction within the last 5 years may result in denial, unless local law requires a different assessment.

## Criminal History Review

Where legally permitted, Northstar may review criminal history. The review considers the nature of the offense, severity, date, rehabilitation evidence, and whether the offense creates a demonstrable risk to residents, staff, or property.

Automatic denial may apply where legally permitted for:

- Conviction involving manufacture or distribution of controlled substances
- Violent felony conviction within the last 7 years
- Sex offense requiring registration
- Arson-related conviction within the last 10 years
- Fraud or identity theft involving housing within the last 7 years

Northstar will follow local fair housing and individualized assessment requirements when applicable.

## Identity Verification

Applicants must pass identity verification before final approval.

Identity verification may include:

- Government ID review
- Selfie or live photo verification
- SSN or ITIN validation
- Address history comparison
- Fraud alert review
- Manual compliance review

If identity cannot be verified, the application may be denied.

## Guarantor Policy

A guarantor may be accepted when an applicant does not meet income or credit requirements.

Guarantor requirements:

- Must submit a separate application
- Must pass identity screening
- Must have gross monthly income of at least 5 times rent
- Must have acceptable credit history
- Must sign the guaranty agreement before move-in
- Must be based in the United States unless management approves otherwise

Guarantors are responsible for rent, damages, fees, utilities, legal fees, and other lease obligations.

## Roommate Applications

Roommates are screened together as a household for income qualification. Each adult must still pass identity, background, and rental history screening.

If one roommate is denied, the household may be denied unless the denied applicant is removed and the remaining applicants qualify independently.

## Conditional Approval Examples

Conditional approval may require one or more of the following:

- Additional deposit
- Approved guarantor
- Deposit alternative enrollment
- Higher risk fee where permitted
- Additional income documentation
- Manager review
- Shorter lease term
- Payment of prior landlord balance

The leasing office must document the condition before lease signing.

## Denial Reasons

An application may be denied for:

- Falsified information
- Fake or altered documents
- Unverifiable identity
- Income below minimum requirement
- Unpaid landlord debt
- Recent eviction history
- Disqualifying criminal history where permitted
- Prior property damage
- Poor landlord reference
- Failure to provide requested documents
- Failure to pay application fees
- Fraud indicators
- Screening provider recommendation

## Adverse Action Notice

If an application is denied or conditionally approved based on consumer report information, the applicant receives an adverse action notice with information about the screening provider and dispute rights.

Leasing staff should not provide legal advice or speculate about credit bureau decisions.
