# Maintenance Service Levels and Work Order Policy

Northstar Residential Management uses a priority-based maintenance process to respond to resident work orders, emergency calls, preventive maintenance, and vendor repairs.

## How Residents Submit Requests

Residents should submit maintenance requests through the resident portal whenever possible. A complete request should include:

- Apartment number
- Room or area affected
- Description of issue
- When the issue started
- Whether there is active leaking, smoke, electrical concern, or safety risk
- Photos or videos when helpful
- Permission to enter preference
- Pet instructions if applicable

Phone requests are accepted for residents who cannot use the portal. Emergency requests must be called in.

## Priority Levels

Maintenance requests are assigned a priority level.

| Priority | Examples | Target Response |
|---|---|---|
| Emergency | Active flood, fire risk, no working toilet in one-bath unit, broken exterior lock | Immediate dispatch |
| Urgent | Refrigerator not cooling, HVAC outage in extreme weather, contained leak | 1 business day |
| Standard | Appliance repair, slow drain, fixture issue, minor leak | 3 business days |
| Routine | Cosmetic repair, cabinet adjustment, screen repair | 5 business days |
| Vendor Required | Flooring, appliance replacement, roofing, specialized trade | Timeline varies |

Response target means initial assessment or dispatch, not guaranteed completion.

## Emergency Maintenance

Emergency maintenance should be called in immediately. Residents should not submit only a portal request for emergencies.

Emergency examples:

- Active flooding or uncontrolled water leak
- Fire, smoke, sparks, or burning electrical smell
- No heat when outside temperature is below 45°F and heat is required by policy or law
- No air conditioning when inside temperature exceeds 85°F and outside temperature exceeds 90°F
- Sewage backup
- Broken exterior door lock
- No working toilet in a one-bathroom apartment
- Broken window or door creating a security issue
- Gas smell
- Carbon monoxide alarm
- Electrical outage affecting only the apartment when not caused by utility provider

Residents should call 911 first for fire, gas leak, medical emergency, active threat, or immediate danger.

## Non-Emergency Issues

These are usually not emergencies:

- Ice maker not working
- Dishwasher not draining
- Garbage disposal jam
- Single burner not working
- Light bulb replacement
- Pest sighting
- Noise complaint
- Locked out of apartment
- Internet outage
- Package locker issue
- Parking dispute
- Cosmetic damage
- Closet door off track

Non-emergency items should be submitted through the resident portal.

## Permission to Enter

Residents may grant permission to enter when submitting a work order. If permission is granted, maintenance may enter during normal maintenance hours.

Normal maintenance hours are Monday through Friday, 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM, excluding property holidays.

If permission is not granted, maintenance will attempt to schedule an appointment. Missed appointments may delay repair.

Emergency maintenance may enter without advance permission when necessary to prevent injury, damage, or serious lease violations.

## Pets During Maintenance

Residents must secure pets before maintenance entry. Pets should be crated, placed in a separate room, or removed from the apartment.

If an unsecured pet prevents safe entry, the work order may be rescheduled.

## Resident-Caused Damage

Residents may be charged for damage caused by misuse, negligence, or failure to report issues promptly.

Examples of chargeable repairs:

| Issue | Typical Charge |
|---|---:|
| Garbage disposal jam from utensils, glass, or non-food items | $95 |
| Toilet clog from wipes or foreign objects | $125 |
| Lock change caused by lost key | $125 |
| Broken blind slat set | $65 |
| Damaged interior door | $175 |
| Carpet stain treatment | $85 per room |
| Appliance damage from misuse | Actual repair cost |
| Water damage from unreported leak | Actual repair cost |

Charges are reviewed by management before posting.

## Appliance Repairs

If an appliance cannot be repaired within 7 business days due to parts availability, the maintenance supervisor reviews replacement options.

Refrigerator issues are prioritized because of food storage. If refrigerator repair requires delay, the property may provide a temporary refrigerator when available.

Northstar does not reimburse for spoiled food unless required by law or approved by regional management.

## HVAC Policy

HVAC requests are prioritized based on weather, indoor temperature, resident vulnerability, and local legal requirements.

Residents should verify the following before submitting a request:

- Thermostat is set correctly
- Breaker is not tripped
- Filter is not blocked
- Windows and doors are closed
- Batteries are working, if thermostat uses batteries

HVAC filters are replaced quarterly at most properties. Residents may be responsible for filter replacement if the lease assigns that duty.

## Water Leak Procedure

For active leaks, residents should:

1. Move personal belongings away from water.
2. Use towels or containers if safe.
3. Turn off local shutoff valves if known and safe.
4. Call emergency maintenance.
5. Submit photos through the resident portal after calling.

Maintenance staff should document source, affected areas, moisture readings, photos, and vendor needs.

## Preventive Maintenance

Northstar performs scheduled preventive maintenance such as:

- HVAC filter replacement
- Smoke detector testing
- Fire extinguisher inspection
- Pest control
- Gutter and drain checks
- Pool equipment inspection
- Exterior lighting checks
- Dryer vent inspection where applicable

Residents receive advance notice when entry is required, except in emergencies.

## Work Order Completion

A work order is completed when the issue is repaired, resolved, referred to a vendor, or closed after resident non-response.

Residents who believe the issue was not resolved should reopen the work order within 72 hours or submit a new request.
